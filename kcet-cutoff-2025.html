Understanding KCET Cutoffs

A KCET cutoff is the last rank at which a seat was allotted in a given college, branch, and category combination. Lower cutoff numbers mean higher competition — a cutoff of 446 for RVCE CSE means only the top 446 rankers in Karnataka could secure that seat.

Cutoffs change every year. Always use the previous year's cutoff as a reference point, not a guarantee. Keep a 10–15% buffer when building your option entry list.

College Profiles

RV College of Engineering (RVCE), Bangalore

NIRF 2025: 101–150 Highest Package: ₹92 LPA KEA Fees: ~₹4.91 Lakhs (total)

RVCE is consistently ranked as the top private engineering college in Karnataka. Located on Mysore Road, Bangalore, the 53-acre campus is managed by the Rashtreeya Sikshana Samithi Trust. In 2024 placements, the highest package was ₹92 LPA. The CSE branch is the most sought after, with an average package of ₹17.2 LPA.

PES University (PESU), Bangalore

NIRF 2025: 101–150 Median B.Tech Package: ₹12.47 LPA KEA Fees: ~₹15.20 Lakhs (total)

PES University attained university status in 2013. It has two main campuses in Bangalore. Out of total B.Tech seats, 40% are filled through KEA KCET counseling. The university is known for strong industry connections, particularly in the software sector.

MS Ramaiah Institute of Technology (MSRIT), Bangalore

NIRF 2025: 75th Highest Package: ₹58 LPA

Ramaiah Institute of Technology is one of Karnataka's most reputed private engineering colleges. In NIRF 2025, MSRIT ranked 75th in Engineering nationally — among the highest of any VTU-affiliated college.

BMS College of Engineering (BMSCE), Bangalore

NIRF 2025: 95th Avg Package: ₹11.4 LPA

Established in 1946, BMSCE is India's first private sector engineering college. The 2025 placement season recorded 383 companies visiting campus with 1,300+ offers made.
